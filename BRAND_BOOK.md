## 1. Brand Foundation

| Element | Official statement |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Name** | PlantPal |
| **Tagline** | Grow with confidence. |
| **One-liner** | PlantPal tells you what your plants are, what's wrong with them, and what to do next. |
| **Mission** | Help every homeowner, gardener, and plant parent grow healthier plants through simple, local, personalized guidance. |
| **Vision** | Become the operating system for plant ownership: every plant gets a digital twin, health profile, growth history, personalized care plan, local climate strategy, and goal-based roadmap. |
| **Promise** | PlantPal gives plant owners **confidence**. Not more information. Not more complexity. |

### What PlantPal is

The **smart plant care coach**. PlantPal helps users know exactly what to do next.

### What PlantPal is not

- A plant encyclopedia you browse for fun
- A generic watering reminder app
- A standalone identification app
- A gardening database or wiki
- An AI demo dressed up as a product

### Mental model for users

**MyFitnessPal for plants and trees.** Track what you own. Get personalized guidance based on plant type, location, climate, and health. Take action daily.

### Brand shorthand

> **Liquid Death meets Duolingo, for plants.**

Confident. Funny. Helpful. Slightly rebellious. Never corporate.

## 2. Positioning & Category

**Category:** Smart plant care coach / plant ownership OS

**Primary differentiators:**

1. **Action over information** — Today page, validated tasks, care logs. Not passive reading.
2. **Local intelligence** — ZIP-based weather, frost alerts, seasonal tasks, climate-adjusted care.
3. **Photo-first diagnosis** — Scanner + Plant Doctor turn symptoms into action plans.
4. **Goal-based growing** — Fruit trees, bonsai, vegetables, houseplants, landscaping. Not one-size-fits-all.
5. **Confidence loop** — Identify → diagnose → plan → task → photo → lesson → streak → repeat.

**Competitive frame (internal only, never in user copy):**

| They optimize for | PlantPal optimizes for |
|-------------------|------------------------|
| Plant ID accuracy | What to do next |
| Encyclopedia depth | Daily action |
| Social feeds | Care outcomes |
| Generic reminders | Validated, contextual tasks |

## 3. Audience

### Primary personas

**The Anxious Plant Parent**  
Owns 5–30 plants. Googles "yellow leaves" at midnight. Wants reassurance and a clear next step.

**The Backyard Grower**  
Fruit trees, vegetables, herbs, zone-aware. Cares about frost, feeding schedules, and harvest timing.

**The Aspirational Landscaper**  
Wants the yard to look intentional. Uses Garden Designer, Property Mode, and seasonal planning.

**The Family Gardener**  
Multiple people, one garden. Shared XP, challenges, and household plant ownership.

**The Learner**  
Academy paths, streaks, badges. Wants to level up without reading a textbook.

### What they all share

- They have killed a plant and felt bad about it
- They do not want to become a horticulture expert
- They want local, practical advice
- They respond to humor, not jargon

## 4. Brand Personality

### Traits (use these)

| Trait | What it means in practice |
|-------|---------------------------|
| **Friendly** | Talk like a knowledgeable neighbor, not a lab coat |
| **Helpful** | Every screen answers "what do I do next?" |
| **Confident** | We know plants. We are not apologetic about it |
| **Slightly rebellious** | Push back on hustle culture, perfectionism, and corporate tone |
| **Funny** | Dry humor, plant drama, gentle roasting. Never mean |
| **Encouraging** | Second chances are core. Plants die. People learn |
| **Smart** | Accurate advice. No fake stats. Label estimates honestly |
| **Nature-first** | Plants and real gardens lead. Tech stays invisible |

### Anti-traits (never use these)

- Corporate
- Overly scientific / academic
- Cold or clinical
- AI buzzword-heavy ("leverage," "ecosystem," "revolutionary")
- Generic gardening store energy
- Preachy or judgmental
- Startup bro

### Reference brands (feel, not copy)

Apple (clarity) + Patagonia (grounded authenticity) + Headspace (calm encouragement) + Duolingo (habit + streaks) + Liquid Death (irreverent confidence)

## 5. Voice & Tone

### Core rules

1. **Short sentences.** If it can be shorter, make it shorter.
2. **Plain language.** No horticulture gatekeeping.
3. **No em dashes.** Use periods or commas instead.
4. **No AI language.** Never "As an AI" or "I'm here to help you with your query."
5. **No startup buzzwords.** See banned list below.
6. **Plants are dramatic.** Lean into it.
7. **Confidence, not fear.** Warn calmly. Never catastrophize.

### Voice: Do

- "Plants are dramatic."
- "Yellow leaves are a cry for help."
- "Let's figure out what's wrong."
- "Your plant isn't dead. Yet."
- "Water it less. Seriously."
- "No green thumb required."
- "Check the soil. Save the drama."

### Voice: Don't

- "Leverage our AI-powered ecosystem."
- "Unlock revolutionary plant insights."
- "A seamless horticultural experience."
- "Empowering plant parents at scale."
- "Best-in-class botanical solutions."
- "Today's horticultural recommendation..."

### Tone by context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Marketing hero | Bold, direct | "Stop killing your plants." |
| Dashboard greeting | Warm, daily | "Good morning. Your garden still has a chance." |
| Planty quip | Funny, short | "The tomatoes are watching." |
| Health diagnosis | Calm, actionable | "Likely overwatering. Let the soil dry before the next drink." |
| Error state | Honest, light | "That didn't work. Plants are dramatic. Apps are too." |
| Frost alert | Urgent but calm | "Frost tonight. Your citrus would like a blanket." |
| Academy lesson | Encouraging teacher | "Small daily learning builds confident plant care habits." |
| Legal / billing | Clear, neutral | No jokes. No Planty. Plain English. |

### Word choices

| Prefer | Avoid |
|--------|-------|
| Plant, garden, grow | Flora, botanical specimen |
| Fix, diagnose, check | Leverage, optimize, synergize |
| Today, next step | Comprehensive, holistic |
| Coach, guide | Platform, solution |
| Confidence | Empowerment |
| Photo, snap | Capture, ingest |

## 7. Visual Identity: Logo

### The Living P

The official PlantPal mark is a **PNG squircle**: white Living P (stem + leaf bowl + vein) on deep botanical green `#2D6A4F`.

**Always use the official file. Never recreate the mark in SVG, Lucide icons, or CSS.**

### Logo variants

| Variant | Use |
|---------|-----|
| **Primary** | Icon tile + "PlantPal" wordmark on light backgrounds |
| **On brand green** | White wordmark + icon on `#2D6A4F` |
| **Dark** | Same on `#111827` or photo overlays |
| **Icon only** | App icon, favicon, avatars, tight spaces |

### Clear space

- Minimum clear space = height of the icon tile on all sides
- Minimum icon size: **24px** digital, **10mm** print
- Minimum full logo width: **100px**. Below that, icon only

### Logo don'ts

- Do not stretch or distort
- Do not recolor the mark
- Do not add drop shadows, glows, or rotation
- Do not substitute generic leaf icons
- Do not use gradients on the mark
- Do not outline or emboss

| Asset | Path |
|-------|------|
| Master app icon | `public/app-icon.png` |
| Next.js favicon | `src/app/icon.png` |

| Source sheet | `assets/brand/planty-source.png` (mascot; separate from logo) |

## 9. Visual Identity: Typography

### Typefaces

| Role | Font | Weights | Use |
|------|------|---------|-----|
| **Headings** | Plus Jakarta Sans | 600 (SemiBold), 700 (Bold) | H1–H3, logo wordmark, marketing headlines, card titles |
| **Body / UI** | Inter | 400 (Regular), 500 (Medium) | Body copy, buttons, labels, metadata |

Both are free Google Fonts, loaded in `src/app/layout.tsx`.

### CSS

- Headings: `.font-heading` or `font-family: var(--font-plus-jakarta)`
- Body: default sans via `--font-inter`

### Type scale (marketing)

| Level | Font | Size | Example |
|-------|------|------|---------|
| H1 | Plus Jakarta Bold | 56–72px | Stop killing your plants. |
| H2 | Plus Jakarta Bold | 32–40px | Plants are dramatic. |
| H3 | Plus Jakarta Bold | 20–24px | Your plant coach in your pocket. |
| Body | Inter Regular | 16–18px | Product descriptions |
| Caption | Inter Medium | 12–14px | Metadata, labels |

### Typography rules

- **Tight tracking** on large headlines (`tracking-tight`)
- **Relaxed leading** on body (`leading-relaxed`)
- **No all-caps body text.** Uppercase only for small labels (10–11px, wide tracking)
- **Sentence case** for buttons and nav
- **Never** use more than two font families

## 11. Visual Identity: Photography & Illustration

### Photography: Use

- Real plants, flaws included (yellow leaves, pests, messy soil)
- Natural lighting, warm tones
- Progress photos: before, during, after
- Real gardens and backyards
- Hands in frame OK ( watering, pruning)

### Photography: Avoid

- Stock business people pointing at plants
- Corporate office plants as hero imagery
- Futuristic sci-fi / glowing circuit boards
- Over-saturated fake green
- Perfect nursery catalog shots only

### Illustration style

- Bright, friendly, Nintendo-quality polish
- Slightly playful, always garden-focused
- Botanically honest (recognizable species)
- Rounded leaves, soft shading

### Planty is the only mascot illustration

Do not create secondary cartoon characters. Do not use generic emoji mascots (🌱 as a character replacement) in product UI where Planty should appear.

## 14. App Product Overview

PlantPal is a **web app (PWA)** with mobile-first UX. Desktop uses sidebar navigation. Phone uses bottom tab bar.

### Core loop

```
Add plant → Get care plan → Daily tasks → Scan/diagnose → Log care →
Learn in Academy → Earn XP → Share with Circles → Repeat
```

### Primary user journeys

1. **New user:** Sign up → Onboarding (ZIP, goals, garden type) → Add first plant → Dashboard
2. **Daily user:** Open app → Planty greeting → Today tasks → Complete validated actions
3. **Problem solver:** Scanner → Diagnose → Recovery plan → Follow-up tasks
4. **Learner:** Academy lesson → Quiz pass → XP + badge → Next lesson
5. **Social:** Friends → Activity feed → Challenge → Shared garden

## 22. Social Media Guidelines

### Post personality

Funny. Helpful. Direct. If it sounds like a press release, delete it.

### Good post examples

- "Your plant isn't dead. Yet."
- "Yellow leaves are a cry for help."
- "Water it less. Seriously."
- "POV: you finally checked the soil"

### Bad post examples

- "Today's horticultural recommendation..."
- "We're excited to announce our AI-powered platform..."
- "Unlock synergistic plant wellness solutions"

### Content pillars

1. **Plant drama** — relatable problems (yellow leaves, pests, overwatering)
2. **Quick wins** — one tip per post
3. **Before/after** — real user progress (with permission)
4. **Academy teasers** — micro-lessons
5. **Seasonal/local** — frost, heat, what to do this week in [region]

### Visuals on social

- Official logo and Planty assets only
- Real plant photos preferred
- Brand green overlays OK
- No off-brand mascots or generic stock

## 24. Legal & Sensitive Contexts

### When to drop the personality

| Context | Treatment |
|---------|-----------|
| Terms of Service | Neutral legal prose |
| Privacy Policy | Neutral, clear |
| Payment / billing | No jokes, no Planty |
| Data export / deletion | Clear instructions |
| Severe plant toxicity warnings | Factual, calm, no mascot |
| Expert review / escalation | Professional, empathetic |

### Health disclaimer framing

PlantPal provides guidance, not professional agricultural or medical advice. Copy should be helpful but not claim to replace arborists, agronomists, or poison control.
